Introduction

This variation on Blood on the Clocktower has the gimmick of deciding roles by asking its players 2 questions, answered from 1 to 5, before starting the match. Depending on how your answers are similar / different to your fellow players decides whether you become a Townsfolk, Outsider or Minion/Demon. The storyteller should aim to pick the Minions & Demon as a group of players with opinions dissimilar to majority but, ideally, similar to each other. Outsiders should be people with an opinion dissimilar to the majority, but not necessarily similar to each other. Townsfolk is everyone who wasn't classified as strange in some way!

Definitions

Question = Asked on the survey at the beginning of the game
Opinion = The value someone gave to one of the questions
Good Opinion = An opinion held by a townsfolk
Evil Opinion = An opinion held by a minion or demon
Someone is "unusual" = They are either an outsider, minion, demon or opinion has registered as like an outsider, minion or demon.

Townsfolk

Washerwoman (Washerwoman Token)

You start knowing 1 of 2 people has a particular good (similar to rest) opinion.

Librarian (Librarian Token)

You start knowing 1 of 2 people are unusual.

Investigator (Investigator Token)

You start knowing 1 of 2 people has a particular evil opinion.

Chef (Chef Token)

You find out how many pairs of unusuals there are

The Host (Empath Token)

At night, hold up a finger from 1 to 3, you find out the opinions of the people next to you on that question.

The Pizzatician (Fortune Teller Token)

You can come up to the storyteller in the day to find out the answer to adding, subtracting or equality of two people's opinions, you cannot ask about your own opinion.

Undercrust (Undertaker Token)

When someone is executed, you find out all of their opinions and their corresponding questions.

Bill Splitter (Monk Token)

Each night*, choose a player (not yourself): If they are attacked by the demon, they learn one opinion of the attacker.

Pigeon (Ravenkeeper Token)

If you die at night, choose one person, you learn all of their opinions and their corresponding questions.

The Picky Eater (Virgin Token)

Choose a question to align to on night 1. If you are nominated by someone with +- 1 as their opinion on that question, they die.

Hater (Slayer Token)

Once per game, you can point at someone and if you know their opinion on one question, they are executed.

Leftover Thief (Soldier Token)

You are safe from the Demon. If you are attacked at night, you learn one opinion and corresponding question of the attacker.

The Franco Manco (Mayor Token)

Once per day, you can gossip and guess everyone's true opinion on one question of your choice. If every opinion is correct, your team wins.

Outsiders

Deliveroo (Butler Token)

One of the townsfolk registers as having your opinions and is classified as an unusual, you know who they are.

Drunk (Drunk Token)

In addition to the usual effect of your power not working, your opinions can also register as whatever the storyteller chooses.

Recluse (Recluse Token)

You might register as having evil opinions, even if dead.

The (Pizza) Pilgrim (Saint Token)

If you die by execution, your team loses.

Minions

Poisoner (Poisoner Token)

Acts normally.

Dominos (Spy Token)

See what everyone submitted to the opinion survey. Your opinions may register as good and you may register as not unusual.

Pizzanovus (Cerenovus Token)

At night, choose one person to make mad for the next day that they have an opinion Thumbs Up (= Love, or 5 on survey), Thumbs Down (=Hate, or 1 on Survey) on one of the questions (hold up a finger from 1 to 2).

Baron (Baron Token)

There are two more outsiders in the game

Imp

Imp (Imp Token)

Acts normally. If there are two or more minions, the imp should be the player with the middle of the evil opinions.
